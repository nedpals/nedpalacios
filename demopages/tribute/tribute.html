Fabrice Bellard

The Ultimate 1000x Programmer

Fabrice Bellard is a programmer you've never seen or heard before.

He is a French programmer known for his greatest contributions in the software industry. He single-handedly created some of the tools we've known today to which any other programmer cannot. Because of this, he was known as one of the 1000x programmers existed in this generation.

Achievments / Contributions

  • He wrote QEMU, the world's most popular open source virtual emulator.
  • He wrote FFmpeg, the world's most popular open source software for multi-media processing.
  • He wrote TCC, a tiny ISO-compliant C compiler that compiles programs in less than 15 seconds.
  • He wrote QuickJS, one of his recent works, which is a small but complete JavaScript engine.
  • He discovered a new, faster formula to calculate single digits of pi known as Bellard's formula.
